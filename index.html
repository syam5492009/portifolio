About

Back in 2006 I started teaching computer science at a university in Vijayawada — and somewhere between grading assignments and building the institute's web portal, I realized I wanted to be on the other side of the keyboard, building things that people actually use.

Fast forward to today, I've had the chance to work on a government hydrology platform used by India's Ministry of Jal Shakti, build AI-powered interview systems using OpenAI's Realtime API, and architect what might be one of the first production MCP Protocol + A2A agent platforms in the industry.

My main focus lately has been at the intersection of full-stack engineering and agentic AI — building systems where autonomous agents actually do real work, not just demos. I care a lot about the difference between a prototype that works in a video and a product that works at 2am in production.

When I'm not pushing code, I'm usually thinking about how AI is going to change the way we build software — and trying to stay ahead of that curve rather than chasing it.
